DesignPath

DesignPath, a responsive web app, is a holistic solution to health and wellness that allows health-conscious female individuals to record and organize their health and medical information and access general physical and mental well-being features.

Overview

Problem

Health-conscious female users need a way to keep track of their medical history and current health status because they need to be aware of how their bodies change over time and to direct their personal healthcare practices accordingly.

Solution

A one-stop app allows users to keep track of their medical history and current health status while planning and implementing their day-to-day personal healthcare goals and health appointments.

Competitive Research

The research was conducted with 3 applications using SWOT profile and 4 main features.

Key Discoveries

  • Unable to find apps that cover all the features.
  • R.W.L houses all your health activity apps in one place, but it lacks any medical features.
  • Ornament digitizes and stores lab results and provides reliable scientific resources using a simple and easy-to-understand platform.
  • Moody Months ' UI is personable and the overall approach is similar to what DesignPath is aiming for.

Target Audience

25 's - 60 's

DesignPath is designed for women between 25-60 years of age who have a little extra time to start caring for their bodies. This age group faces dramatic body changes through pregnancy, aging, and the menopausal stage. Keeping track of their body can ease the challenges they encounter during this time. DesignPath hopes to widen the age demographic gradually to anyone above 16 to 70+ women who have regular health check-ups.

Opportunity

One stop app for health & wellness solution

DesignPath is the only app on the market that includes all of the features of ORGANIZE, RECORD, SHARE, and LEARN. Ornament and RWL specialize in recording medical data and offering physical activities. Moody Month has successfully designed a user-centred app that attracts users to visit every day for their wellness activities. DesignPath aims to incorporate these strong points and Moody Month UI with specialized medical and wellness features.

User Research

Conducted 4 user interviews and determined the research goals by analyzing the data using the affinity mapping.

Research Goals

  1. To better understand users' daily routines around health and wellness activities.
  2. To determine users' interest and perspective in recording and storing medical and prescription history/information.
  3. To explore users' needs and pain points using health and wellness apps/websites.

Discovery

  1. To find a way to store health data of different treatment methods outside the conventional medical system.
  2. To make the health calendar feature the hub of the app so that users can keep track of their health events daily.

Research Insights

Recording/Storing

  • People are looking for one app to record and store their medical and health data including varieties of treatment methods outside of the medical system.

Learning

  • The health and wellness reading materials and recipes should come from multiple sources to keep people attracted to the app.
  • Fitness and mindset exercise materials should be able to be customized to fit users' needs with an option for descriptive audio.

Daily Activities

  • A calendar for daily activities with reminders can help people achieve their daily activity goals.
  • A visible monthly summary (activity analytic dashboard) can help users motivate and keep a long-term activity routine.

Organize/Make Appointments

  • Creating a calendar with all the doctors' and health-related contact info can help users quickly make appointments with reminders and make it easy to see their overall medical and health schedule.

Motivation

  • 85% of interviewees are highly motivated and focused on their well-being, especially physical health and nutrition.
  • Supplements intake with reminders embedded into the calendar will help to keep a routine.

Preference

  • Offering offline content is a great asset.
  • Provide various screen size options for exercise content.
  • Provide various categories/styles of exercises.

Usability Testing

Conducted usability testing with 6 users using the remotely moderated method of research. The test included a short briefing and 3 task performances using DesignPath on a mobile app and found 3 key issues.

Test Results Overview

100%

  • Completed the usability test without any significant errors.
  • Understood the purpose of this application by observation.
  • Gave positive feedback.

100%

Evidence

Participants spent the most time searching for the next action on the My Data screen.

Solution

Bring up the most used category to the top to reduce the user's cognitive load.

50%

Evidence

Participants asked if they could customize the home screen.

Solution

Adding a customized button creates an option for users to tailor the app to their needs.

50%

Evidence

Participants gravitated to "+" button when asked to enter the lab data.

Solution

Add categories (calendar, my data, my doctor, my health) inside the Log(+) icon.
